Bio

I am a CS PhD student at the Matter of Tech Lab, working to advance AAC technologies. My research focuses on integrating non-informative speech, such as humor and interjections, to enhance expressivity for individuals with speech and motor impairments.

Research Interests: Accessibility, AAC, expressive communication, LLMs, human-AI interaction, HCI.

Publications

Why So Serious? Exploring Humor in AAC Through AI-Powered Interfaces

Authors: Tobias Weinberg, Kowe Kadoma, Ricardo E. Gonzalez Penuela, Stephanie Valencia, Thijs Roumen

Conference: CHI 2025 (In submission)

This study explores how AI-powered AAC interfaces can help users with speech disabilities deliver timely, humorous comments. It highlights design insights to improve expressivity and timing in AAC technology.
